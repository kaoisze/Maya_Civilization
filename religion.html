Religion & Culture

Beliefs

The Mayas religion involved several aspects of nature, astronomy and rituals.


Most gods they worshipped represented nature, and the Mayas built temples/shrines for them. They also were polytheistic and worshipped more than 165 gods.

The Maya viewed the cosmos as structured. There were thirteen levels in the heavens and nine in the underworld, with the human world between both.

Rituals

The sacrifice of a human life was the ultimate offering to the gods. Generally only high status prisoners of war were sacrificed(Kings, leaders, etc). At the core of Maya religious practice was the worship of deceased ancestors.

In addition, the Maya used these rituals to appeal to the gods for things such as a good harvest, weather, fertility, war, and much more.

Pok-Ta-Pok

Pok-Ta-Pok is a ceremonial ball-game(click here for video) that was played by the Mayas

The game was played for fun, during festivals or special events such as a ruler becoming king, or to replace war.


There is mythology regarding the game and it is about the hero twins who fought the lords of the underworld and revived their father.
