Art


Maya art was typically reserved for nobles and served to connect the Maya to their ancestors, gods, and stories.

Maya blue is a vibrant azure blue pigment created by pre-Columbian Mesoamerican cultures. It first appeared in paintings in 800 AD and was widely used.

Stelae

Maya stone sculpture emerged into the archaeological record as a fully developed tradition. This suggests there may have been a predecessor form of creating these arts, but there is no conclusive information.

Most notably, Stone Maya stelae were widespread in city sites, often paired with low, circular stones referred to as altars in the literature. They were carefully carved and used as decoration in important temples/pyramids to honor the gods or rulers.


Paintings

The Maya had a long tradition of mural painting and used a variety of materials to create wondrous pieces of art. The majority of these murals have been destroyed, but Early Classic tombs painted in cream, red, and black have been excavated and put in museums. Some well-preserved murals can be seen at Bonampak.


Pottery

Besides carrying food, drinks, and accessories, Maya pottery was used as a canvas to tell stories, honor the Gods, remembering the dead, and more regarding Maya tradition and culture.
