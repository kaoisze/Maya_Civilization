Art


Maya art was generally only for nobles and served to link the Maya to their ancestors, gods, and stories.

Maya blue is a bright azure blue pigment manufactured by cultures of pre-Columbian Mesoamerica. It was used commonly in paintings and first appeared in 800 AD.

Stelae

Maya stone sculpture emerged into the archaeological record as a fully developed tradition. This suggests that there may have been a predecessor form of creating these arts, but none have been found.

Most notably, Stone Maya stelae were widespread in city sites, often paired with low, circular stones referred to as altars in the literature. They were carefully carved and used as decoration in important temples/pyramids to honor the gods or rulers.


Paintings

The Maya had a long tradition of mural painting and used a variety of materials to create wondorous pieces of art. The majority of these murals have been destroyed, but Early Classic period tombs painted in cream, red, and black have been excavated and put in museums. Among the best preserved murals, they can be seen at Bonampak


Pottery

Maya pottery was used as a canvas to tell stories, honor the Gods, remember the dead and more regarding Maya tradition and culture.
